## Design Context

### Users

People who use a Chrome extension to save, inspect, organize, export, import, and restore browser sessions. They interact with the product inside a narrow popup and need to understand controls quickly without losing focus on their browsing task.

### Brand Personality

Orderly, reliable, compact. The interface should feel calm and deliberate, with visible structure and no ornamental clutter.

### Aesthetic Direction

A refined utility interface built around the existing light and dark themes, blue accent, softly tinted surfaces, restrained borders, and compact typography. Settings should favor clear semantic grouping, left alignment, consistent control sizing, and tight spacing within groups with stronger separation between groups. Avoid decorative gradients, excessive shadows, nested cards, oversized headings, and generic dashboard styling.

### Design Principles

1. Group by user intent: language, appearance, backup, support, and resources remain visually distinct.
2. Keep related controls close and separate unrelated tasks with space, borders, or surface changes.
3. Preserve density without crowding; every label and line should earn its space.
4. Maintain keyboard focus visibility, readable contrast, and equivalent light/dark behavior.
5. Reuse the extension's existing visual language before introducing new patterns.
